JOLLOF RICE

Description

Jollof Rice is a popular West African dish made with rice cooked in a rich, flavorful tomato sauce. It typically includes ingredients such as tomatoes, onions, red bell peppers, and a blend of spices that give it its distinctive reddish-orange color and smoky taste. The dish is often served with fried plantains, grilled chicken, or fish. Known for its vibrant flavor and cultural significance, Jollof Rice is a staple at parties, family gatherings, and celebrations across many African countries, each with its own unique twist on the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of long-grain parboiled rice
  • 4 large tomatoes (or 1 can of blended tomatoes)
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1–2 Scotch bonnet (ata rodo) or chili peppers (for heat)
  • 1 large onion (half for blending, half for frying)
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2–3 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2–3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (optional)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 2 seasoning cubes (e.g., Maggi or Knorr)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable or sunflower oil
  • Cooked chicken, beef, or fish (optional, for serving)

Preparation Steps

  1. Blend the sauce: Blend the tomatoes, red bell peppers, Scotch bonnet, and half of the onion into a smooth puree. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the base: Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the remaining chopped onions and fry until fragrant and slightly golden.
  3. Add tomato paste: Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2–3 minutes. This helps deepen the flavor and color of the rice.
  4. Add blended mixture: Pour in the blended tomato-pepper mixture. Cook on medium heat for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and the raw tomato taste disappears.
  5. Season the sauce: Add thyme, curry powder, bay leaves, paprika (if using), seasoning cubes, and salt. Stir well to combine.
  6. Add stock and rice: Pour in the chicken or vegetable stock, then add the washed rice. Stir so the sauce coats the rice evenly.
  7. Cook the rice: Cover the pot tightly with foil or a lid to trap steam. Cook on low heat for about 25–30 minutes. Avoid stirring too often to prevent the rice from becoming mushy.
  8. Check and finish: When the rice is soft and the liquid absorbed, gently stir to combine everything evenly. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  9. Serve and enjoy: Serve hot with fried plantains, grilled chicken, beef, or fish.
